Best ceramic coating options for 2025: What’s new and what works

Ceramic coatings have become the gold standard in vehicle paint protection. With 2025 rolling in, a host of new formulations and innovations have emerged that push the limits of durability, gloss, and environmental resistance. Whether you’re a professional detailer or a DIY enthusiast, knowing which ceramic coating delivers real performance is essential.

Below is a focused look at the top four ceramic coating brands in 2025, highlighting what’s new, what’s improved, and what actually works.

1. IGL Coatings Kenzo

IGL Coatings’ flagship product, Kenzo, is a high-performance, graphene-reinforced ceramic coating designed for professional use. Kenzo provides an ultra-thick 10H-rated protective layer that delivers up to four years of gloss and chemical resistance. It’s well-known for its luxurious “wet-look” finish and extreme hydrophobicity. What sets Kenzo apart in 2025 is its upgraded low-VOC, eco-friendly formulation, certified under ISO standards and REACH compliance.

Key benefits:

  • Graphene-reinforced 10H hardness
  • Eco-friendly and low-VOC formulation
  • Professional-only application
  • Deep, candy-gloss appearance

2. Gtechniq Crystal Serum Ultra

Known for long-term durability and professional-grade hardness, Gtechniq Crystal Serum Ultra continues to be a go-to in 2025. It offers up to nine years of paint protection with a 10H hardness rating. Its dual-layer technology provides flexibility, preventing cracking and ensuring consistent protection.

Key benefits:

  • Up to nine years of durability
  • Dual-layer protection
  • High resistance to chemicals and UV

3. CarPro CQuartz UK 3.0

A staple in the detailing world, CarPro CQuartz UK 3.0 is popular for its accessibility to both professionals and enthusiasts. The latest 2025 formula offers improved water spot resistance and easier application in humid or cold conditions. It also features a brilliant candy-like gloss and strong protection for up to two to three years.

Key benefits:

  • Easy DIY application
  • Deep gloss finish
  • Improved weather tolerance

4. Feynlab Self-Heal Plus

Feynlab Self-Heal Plus brings unique self-healing properties into the ceramic coating space. Light surface swirls can self-correct under heat, making it a favorite among enthusiasts who want to preserve showroom finishes. It also includes advanced UV and chemical resistance.

Key benefits:

  • Heat-activated self-healing
  • 5-year protection
  • High-gloss, slick finish

2025 ceramic coating trends to watch

  • Graphene integration: In 2025, graphene continues to revolutionize ceramic coatings by improving not only heat resistance but also adding anti-static properties and better chemical bonding to surfaces. This results in smoother application, longer durability, and enhanced gloss depth.
  • Eco-friendly formulas: Sustainability is no longer optional. Many of the top ceramic coating brands are adopting water-based carriers, reducing VOC emissions, and ensuring REACH and ISO compliance. These formulations are safer for installers and the environment without compromising performance.

2025 ceramic coating trends to watch

  • Smart coatings: Technological advancements have led to smart coatings that adapt to their environment. This includes UV tracer dyes that glow under black light for proper application coverage, and polymers that heal minor abrasions when exposed to heat or sunlight. These features increase product longevity and reduce maintenance needs.
  • Ease of application for pros and DIYers: New coatings are designed with more forgiving application windows, faster curing times, and compatibility with a wider range of surfaces. This democratizes high-end paint protection, allowing both professionals and enthusiasts to achieve stunning, long-lasting results.
